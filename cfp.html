Call for Papers

We invite submissions to LT4CPR: Language Technologies for Crisis Preparedness and Response, a 1-day workshop at AACL 2026. The workshop aims to advance language and speech technologies for crisis preparedness, disaster risk reduction, and crisis response—bridging NLP, local languages, and real-world humanitarian impact.

Topics

We welcome work at the intersection of language technology and crisis preparedness/response, including (but not limited to):

  • Multilingual / low-resource NLP and speech for crisis scenarios
  • Machine translation and interpreting support for humanitarian response
  • Crisis informatics, social media analysis, and information triage
  • Information extraction, event detection, and situation awareness
  • Summarization and report generation for operational decision-making
  • Human-in-the-loop systems, evaluation, and deployment considerations

Shared Task: Automatic Situation Report (“Sitrep”) Generation

We will include a shared task on automatic situation report generation from social media content pertaining to crisis scenarios. Situation reports (“sitreps”) are commonly compiled manually and issued within the first 24 hours of a crisis and then daily afterwards; partially automating this process could bring significant real-world value.

Submission

  • ARR (primary track for NLP papers): We will accept papers submitted via ARR. (Submission link and ARR cycle details will be posted here.)
  • Softconf (for papers outside of NLP): We will also accept submissions through Softconf, primarily to support relevant work coming from outside the NLP community. (Softconf link and instructions will be posted here.)

Presentation and Publication

Accepted papers will be presented at the workshop. We also welcome contributions from allied fields and practitioners; some contributions may be invited as talks rather than archival publications (details will be clarified in the final CFP).
